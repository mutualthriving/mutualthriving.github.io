Who We Are

🌱

Individual Thriving

A world that allows us to thrive as individuals: have our basic needs met, seek meaningful fulfillment, find pleasure in life and self-actualize.

🤝

Collective Flourishing

Our actions are compatible with the flourishing of others, fostering win/win dynamics instead of zero-sum competition.

🌍

Future Generations

Our choices stay within planetary boundaries, ensuring future generations can thrive too.

Mutual Thriving is a movement driven by the paradigm that the best life we can live is one that fosters our thriving in harmony with the thriving of all.

We coordinate knowledge exchange between communities around the world to help new ones establish more easily. Our vision is a paradigm shift on a civilizational level, from a game-theoretic (win/lose) worldview to one that has the flourishing of all in mind, starting with yourself.

How to Be a Thriver

Being a thriver is a skill to be honed. Scrutinize your choices and activities with these 3 decision criteria:

1

Does it help me thrive?

No moral obligation to do things that significantly hurt your thriving.

2

Is it sustainably generalizable?

Could what you do get adopted by basically anyone on the planet without putting the thriving of future generations at risk?

3

Does it help others thrive?

Or at least be invariant to other people's thriving.

Choose actions with 3 YESes. If you have found actions with 3 YESes, you can have high confidence that your action lies within the mutual thriving criteria. Congratulations, you have found a mutual thriving format!

An Introduction to Mutual Thriving

Motivation

Our current system is misaligned:

  • Our current global society is failing to mitigate existential risk. Rather, we are racing towards catastrophe (AI arms race, climate crisis, etc.)
  • Despite living in historically unmatched wealth, individuals of the Western World are getting more depressed and anxious than ever before.

How can we steer society into a more aligned direction? We argue:

  1. We need a shift on a paradigmatic level.
  2. We will have to do it bottom-up, because top-down change is currently not expectable.

Wanted: Paradigm Shift

Parameter changes (like making machines more efficient) and even new goals (like the Sustainable Development Goals) are not enough if the underlying paradigm stays misaligned. We need change at a paradigmatic level: How we view the world, how we view ourselves in relationship with the world, what we value in ourselves and others.

Win/Lose thinking → Win/Win thinking: The most important shift to accomplish is a transition from win/lose thinking to win/win thinking. Away from the game-theoretic modeling of our world as a zero-sum game towards a mindset of abundance, with the worldview that our own thriving and that of others are compatible—in many cases even in a symbiotic relationship with each other.

We Cannot Rely on Top-Down Change

Governments are trapped in a global competition for wealth and political power. Sustainable measures are a classic multi-polar trap, where win/lose assumptions incentivize any player to prioritize short-term gains over long-term sustainability.

Actionable Bottom-up Change: We need a bottom-up approach that is actionable. Start with a local community who successfully implements the new paradigm, attract new members so that the community becomes a movement, and reach a critical mass so that culture shift can happen.

Vision: Mutual Thriving Paradigm

"How can I live a life that's good for me and good for others?"

We believe that a world worth striving for fulfills three criteria:

  1. It allows us to thrive as individuals: Have our basic needs met, foster inter-personal relationships, find pleasure in life and self-actualize.
  2. Our individual actions, as well as our collective actions, are compatible with the flourishing of others.
  3. Our individual actions, as well as our collective actions, are compatible with the flourishing of future generations.

That's it! If we can agree on these three criteria as core criteria for a successful civilization, we have established a framework for a win/win culture.

A Better Way to Live

"Culture change happens when a group of people has found a better way to live."

What if you could be part of a culture that lets you keep the good parts of your life but reinforces you to pursue things that actually make you more fulfilled?

Foster expressions of the mutual thriving paradigm that are beneficial even from the perspective of the current cultural paradigm:

  • Improve financial freedom
  • Improve individual health & well-being
  • Improve body aesthetics
  • Improve intimacy & connection

These gains are totally attainable in a mutually beneficial way—you just need to be creative. Think of going for walks with friends instead of drinks → save money, improve health. Think of community mealprep sessions → save money, eat healthy, meet people in person.
