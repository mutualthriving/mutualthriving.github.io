The Principles of Mutual Thriving

Where do we want to go as a civilization?
We believe that a world worth striving for fulfils the following criteria:

1. Self

It allows us to thrive as individuals: have our basic needs met, seek meaningful fulfillment, find pleasure in life and self-actualize.

2. Others

Our actions (individual and collective) are compatible with the flourishing of all.

3. Future

Our actions (individual and collective) are compatible with the flourishing of future generations.

If we satisfy these criteria, we end up in a world of "mutual thriving" as a global family.

We argue that a life according to the win/win principles of mutual thriving can represent not only a morally good life, but a better way to live even from the old paradigm's perspective.

How to Be a Thriver

Being a thriver is a skill to be honed. Scrutinize your choices and activities with these 3 decision criteria:

1

Does it help me thrive?

No moral obligation to do things that significantly hurt your thriving.

2

Is it sustainably generalizable?

Could what you do get adopted by basically anyone on the planet without putting the thriving of future generations at risk?

3

Does it help others thrive?

Or at least be invariant to other people's thriving.

Choose actions with 3 YESes.

If you have found actions with 3 YESes, you can have high confidence that your action lies within the mutual thriving criteria. Congratulations, you have found a mutual thriving format!
